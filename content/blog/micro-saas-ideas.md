## Introduction

Micro SaaS—small, focused SaaS products addressing specific niches—have become increasingly popular. Why? Because the barriers to entry are lower than ever, and there's a market for thousands of specialized solutions.

A successful micro SaaS can generate $2,000-10,000 monthly recurring revenue (MRR) with a solo founder or small team. Many have grown to 6-7 figures annually.

This guide explores profitable micro SaaS ideas across different categories, along with evaluation criteria to assess viability.

## What Makes a Good Micro SaaS Idea?

Before diving into specific ideas, understand what makes a micro SaaS successful:

### 1. Solves Real Problem

The problem must be painful enough that people will pay to solve it.

### 2. Niche Market

Target a specific, well-defined market rather than trying to serve everyone.

### 3. Low Marginal Costs

Recurring revenue with low delivery costs creates healthy unit economics.

### 4. Can Be Built Quickly

MVP in 2-6 months, not years. Scope ruthlessly.

### 5. Recurring Revenue

Subscription model provides predictable revenue.

### 6. Low Churn

Solve a problem customers need regularly, not one-time.

### 7. High Retention

Customers see ongoing value and stay subscribed.

### 8. Defensible

Hard to copy, either through brand, data, or network effects.

## Evaluating Micro SaaS Ideas

### Problem Validation

**Ask These Questions**:
- Would you solve this problem yourself?
- Do people currently pay for a solution?
- How much would they pay?
- Is the problem painful enough to motivate action?

**Validation Method**: Talk to 20 people with the problem. Do they care? Would they pay?

### Market Size Validation

**Estimate addressable market**:
- How many people have this problem?
- What percentage would actually use SaaS solution?
- What's realistic capture rate?

**Rule of thumb**: Market should be at least 10,000 potential customers for viable micro SaaS.

### Competitive Analysis

**Check**:
- Who else is solving this?
- How much do they charge?
- What are weaknesses?
- Can you differentiate?

**Caution**: Crowded market means demand exists but hard to break in.

### Technical Feasibility

**Can you build it**?
- What technologies needed?
- How long is realistic MVP timeline?
- Do you have skills or can you learn?
- Can you launch in 3-6 months?

**If MVP takes 12+ months, scope is too large.**

### Revenue Potential

**Model economics**:
- Average deal size × expected customers in year 1 = potential ARR
- Typical micro SaaS targets: $500-2,000 MRR year 1

**Example**:
- $29/month price
- 20 customers in month 3 (ramp up)
- 50 customers by month 12
- Year 1 = ~$15,000 revenue

Not huge but viable for solo founder.

## Validation Playbook

### Phase 1: Problem Validation (2 weeks)

1. Identify target customer
2. Have 20 conversations asking about problem
3. Assess if people care enough

**Go/No-Go**: Are people excited about solving this?

### Phase 2: Solution Validation (2-4 weeks)

1. Create mockup or landing page
2. Show 50 potential customers
3. Track interest and willingness to pay

**Go/No-Go**: Would people actually pay?

### Phase 3: Build MVP (2-3 months)

1. Scope ruthlessly (10 features max)
2. Build core functionality only
3. Get working MVP to real users

**Go/No-Go**: Can you build something useful?

### Phase 4: Customer Discovery (1 month)

1. Get MVP to 5-10 paying customers
2. Iterate based on feedback
3. Confirm product-market fit signals

**Go/No-Go**: Are customers using and paying?

### Phase 5: Scale (Month 6+)

If Phase 4 validates, scale acquisition and optimize.

## Common Micro SaaS Mistakes

### Building Without Validation

Don't build for months without talking to users. Validate before building.

### Choosing Too Broad Market

"For anyone" means "for nobody." Choose specific, narrow market.

### Underpricing

Founders often price at $9/month when market would bear $49/month. Test higher prices.

### Ignoring Customer Acquisition

Building a great product is necessary, not sufficient. Focus on getting customers from day one.

### Chasing Trends

Don't build whatever is trendy. Build solutions to real problems you understand.

### No Differentiation

Don't build if 5 competitors already exist. Find gap or differentiation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much money do you need to start a micro SaaS?
Almost nothing if you have the skills. $1,000-5,000 to cover landing page hosting, payment processing. More if hiring contractors.

### Can I build and launch in 3 months?
Yes, if you scope aggressively and have necessary skills. Focus on core features only.

### What if someone copies my idea?
Hard to execute well. First movers and marketing create moat. Speed and iteration matter more than idea.

### Should I work on micro SaaS full-time or part-time?
Start part-time while validating. Full-time once you have paying customers and confidence in idea.

### What's realistic year 1 revenue?
$500-2,000 MRR if executed well. Some hit $5,000+ MRR if they nail market and product.

## Conclusion

Micro SaaS provides an accessible path to building a profitable business with limited resources. The key is choosing a specific problem, validating demand, building a focused MVP, and iterating quickly.

The advantages of micro SaaS:
- Lower risk and investment
- Faster to profitability
- Easier to validate ideas
- Can be solo or small team
- Real revenue potential

Start by identifying a problem you understand deeply and that you'd solve for yourself. Validate it with potential customers. Build an MVP. Get to paying customers. Iterate and optimize.

Many successful SaaS companies started as micro SaaS ideas. With patience and focus, your micro SaaS could too.
